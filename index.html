Birds of Colour

A photographic field collection of Sri Lanka's most vibrant avifauna — from endemic jewels to seasonal migrants — documented in the wild.

Coraciiformes
01
Blue-tailed Bee-eater
Merops philippinus
Meropidae Resident / Migrant
A slender, jewel-toned aerial hunter of open skies — sweeps and glides over paddy fields, rivers and forest clearings.
→
02
Indian Roller
Coracias benghalensis
Coraciidae Resident
Perched on a wire it appears unremarkable — until it rolls and tumbles through the air, flashing brilliant electric blue.
→
03
Oriental Dollarbird
Eurystomus orientalis
Coraciidae Resident
Named for the pale blue–white circular wing patches that flash like silver coins in flight.
→
04
White-throated Kingfisher
Halcyon smyrnensis
Alcedinidae Resident
One of Sri Lanka's most familiar and boldly coloured birds — commands attention with its dazzling turquoise plumage.
→
05
Oriental Dwarf Kingfisher
Ceyx erithaca
Alcedinidae Resident
A living jewel of the rainforest understory — among the world's most dazzling small birds.
→
Trogoniformes
06
Malabar Trogon
Harpactes fasciatus
Trogonidae Resident
A resplendent gem of the rainforest canopy — one of Asia's most visually striking birds.
→
Passeriformes
07
Small Minivet
Pericrocotus cinnamomeus
Campephagidae Resident
Males flash brilliant orange-red against slate-grey; females a delicate yellow — restless canopy birds of open forest.
→
08
Indian Paradise Flycatcher
Terpsiphone paradisi
Monarchidae Migrant
The male trails extraordinary ribbon-like tail streamers, drifting through dappled forest shade like a ghost.
→
09
Indian Pitta
Pitta brachyura
Pittidae Migrant
A jewel of the forest floor — dazzles with nine vivid colours, earning the local name navaranga ("nine-coloured").
→
10
Loten's Sunbird
Cinnyris lotenius
Nectariniidae Endemic
A jewel-bright resident of Sri Lanka's gardens and forest edges with an unusually long, decurved bill.
→
11
Sri Lanka Swallow
Cecropis hyperythra
Hirundinidae Endemic
Sri Lanka's only endemic swallow — deep chestnut underparts and glossy steel-blue back, sweeping effortlessly over paddy fields and wetlands.
→
12
Sri Lanka Blue-Magpie
Urocissa ornata
Corvidae Endemic
Chestnut body, cobalt-blue wings and tail, vivid red bill and legs — a spectacular corvid of the wet zone hill forests found nowhere else on earth.
→
Bucerotiformes
13
Malabar Pied Hornbill
Anthracoceros coronatus
Bucerotidae Resident
Bold black-and-white plumage, a massive casqued bill — heard long before it is seen, crashing through the forest canopy.
→
Psittaciformes
14
Sri Lanka Hanging Parrot
Loriculus beryllinus
Psittaculidae Endemic
A tiny, acrobatic jewel found only in Sri Lanka — earns its name from the extraordinary habit of roosting upside-down.
