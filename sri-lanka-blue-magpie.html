Sri Lanka Blue-Magpie

Urocissa ornata

Weight 165–200 g
Length 42–47 cm
Wingspan ~52 cm
Endemic · Sri Lanka

One of Sri Lanka's most spectacular endemic birds — the Sri Lanka Blue-Magpie is a vibrant, long-tailed corvid of the island's montane rainforests. Its extraordinary combination of rich chestnut wings and body, brilliant cobalt-blue plumage and vivid red bill and legs make it one of the most visually striking birds in all of South Asia. Inquisitive, noisy and highly social, it moves through the forest understorey in boisterous family parties.

Field Notes
🌿
Habitat
Montane Rainforest
Dense primary and mature secondary wet evergreen montane forest, typically above 500 m elevation. Strongly associated with the island's wet zone hill forests.
📍
Distribution
Sri Lanka Only
Strictly endemic — confined to the wet zone hill forests of Sri Lanka, primarily in the central highlands and Knuckles range. Absent from the dry zone.
⚖️
Weight & Size
165–200 g · 42–47 cm
A large, long-tailed corvid. The graduated tail accounts for more than half the bird's total length, trailing dramatically behind it in flight.
🍄
Diet
Omnivore
Highly varied diet: insects, lizards, frogs, small snakes, eggs and nestlings, fruit and berries. Often raids the nests of other bird species.
🔊
Voice
Loud, varied calls
A repertoire of raucous, scolding calls — loud kaa-kaa notes, harsh chattering and melodious whistles. Noisy and conspicuous when moving through the understorey in flocks.
🔴
Conservation
Vulnerable
IUCN Vulnerable. Threatened by ongoing deforestation and degradation of the wet zone hill forests. Sensitive to forest disturbance; rarely persists in heavily modified habitats.
About this Species

Appearance

The Sri Lanka Blue-Magpie (Urocissa ornata) is a large, brilliantly coloured corvid measuring 42–47 cm, with a long graduated tail adding dramatically to its presence. The head, neck and upper mantle are deep chestnut-rufous, contrasting sharply with the vivid cobalt-blue wings, tail and lower body. The tail is tipped white, and the long graduated feathers create an elegant trailing silhouette in flight. The bill, orbital ring and legs are all a striking bright red — giving the bird an almost theatrical colour palette that is immediately unmistakable.

The sexes are alike in plumage. Juveniles are duller overall — browner above, with a less vivid blue — and gradually acquire full adult colouration over their first year. The eye is pale yellowish-white in adults, adding to the bird's striking facial expression.

Behaviour & Social Life

The Sri Lanka Blue-Magpie is a highly social and inquisitive bird, almost always found in noisy, active family parties of four to eight individuals that move together through the forest in a loose, straggling procession. The group communicates constantly with a variety of loud calls, and individuals frequently mob potential predators with raucous scolding.

Like all corvids, it is highly intelligent and opportunistic. It forages at all levels of the forest — from the ground litter to the mid-canopy — turning over leaves, probing bark and investigating crevices in search of prey. It is known to follow other foraging animals such as giant squirrels to steal food, and will readily plunder the nests of other birds for eggs and chicks.

Breeding

Breeding occurs from February to May, coinciding with the pre-monsoon dry period in the hills. The nest is a large, untidy cup of twigs, lined with fine roots and leaves, placed in a tree fork in dense forest, often at a considerable height. The clutch is typically three to five pale greenish eggs spotted with brown. Cooperative breeding behaviour has been observed, with non-breeding group members helping to feed the nestlings — a trait shared with other corvid species.
